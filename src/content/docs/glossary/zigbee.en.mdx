## What is Zigbee?

**Zigbee** is a low-power, low-data-rate wireless mesh networking standard (IEEE 802.15.4) designed for personal area networks. It is one of the most popular protocols for smart home devices.

## Key Features

- **Mesh networking**: Mains-powered devices relay messages, extending range
- **Low power**: Battery-operated sensors can last years
- **Frequency**: Operates on 2.4 GHz (global), 868 MHz (EU), 915 MHz (US)
- **Range**: 10–20 m per hop, extended by the mesh

## Common Zigbee Devices

- Smart bulbs (Philips Hue, IKEA DIRIGERA)
- Motion and occupancy sensors
- Door and window contact sensors
- Smart plugs and switches
- Temperature and humidity sensors

## Zigbee Coordinators

To use Zigbee devices, you need a **coordinator** (hub):

- **Zigbee2MQTT** with a USB coordinator stick
- **ZHA** (Zigbee Home Automation) integration in Home Assistant
- **Philips Hue Bridge**
- **Amazon Echo** (4th gen and later, built-in Zigbee)
