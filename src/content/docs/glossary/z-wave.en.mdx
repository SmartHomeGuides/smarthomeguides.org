## What is Z-Wave?

**Z-Wave** is a wireless communication protocol designed specifically for home automation. Unlike Zigbee and Wi-Fi, Z-Wave operates on sub-GHz frequencies (800–900 MHz range), which reduces interference and improves wall penetration.

## Key Features

- **Sub-GHz frequency**: 868 MHz (EU), 908 MHz (US) — avoids 2.4 GHz congestion
- **Mesh networking**: Up to 232 devices in a single network
- **Interoperability**: Z-Wave certification guarantees cross-brand compatibility
- **Low power**: Battery devices last months to years
- **Range**: 30–100 m per hop outdoors, less indoors

## Z-Wave vs. Zigbee

| Feature       | Z-Wave           | Zigbee                     |
| ------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------- |
| Frequency     | Sub-GHz          | 2.4 GHz                    |
| Max devices   | 232              | 65,000+                    |
| Interference  | Low              | Higher (shares with Wi-Fi) |
| Certification | Mandatory        | Optional                   |
| Ecosystem     | Smaller, curated | Larger, varied             |

## Getting Started with Z-Wave

You need a **Z-Wave controller** (USB stick or hub):

- **Z-Wave JS** in Home Assistant
- **Aeotec Z-Stick** or **Zooz ZST39** USB controllers
- **SmartThings Hub** (built-in Z-Wave)
- **Hubitat Elevation** (built-in Z-Wave)
